Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone

Director: Chris Columbus

Genre: Family/Fantasy

Year Released: 2001

Awards: Young Artist Award for Best Leading Young Actress in a Feature Film
Fun Fact: The chess pieces in the life-sized Wizard’s Chess scene were actually real, full-sized, moving models — not CGI!

Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone is a movie based on a book of the sane name by J.K. Rowling.

The movie follows a young boy, Harry Potter, who discovers on his eleventh birthday that he is a wizard. After years of mistreatment by his cruel aunt and uncle, he’s invited to attend Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. There, he makes close friends—Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger—and learns about the magical world, his mysterious past, and the dark wizard Voldemort who killed his parents.

When Harry discovers that someone is trying to steal the powerful Philosopher’s Stone, he and his friends must stop them, leading to a confrontation that reveals Harry’s courage and the lingering threat of Voldemort’s return.
